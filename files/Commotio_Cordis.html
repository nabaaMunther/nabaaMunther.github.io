Commotio Cordis - Nelson Explanation

Definition

Commotio cordis is sudden cardiac death due to ventricular fibrillation that occurs after a blunt, nonpenetrating blow to the chest (precordium).

Mechanism

- The blow causes a sudden increase in intracardiac pressure. - If the impact occurs during a critical 10–20 ms window of ventricular repolarization (just before the peak of the T wave), ventricular fibrillation is triggered. - No structural damage occurs to the heart, vessels, or chest wall.

Epidemiology

- Most cases occur in adolescent boys (~15 years old, >90% male). - Commonly seen in sports: baseball, hockey, lacrosse, football. - Victims typically have no underlying cardiac disease.

Clinical Presentation

- Immediate collapse within seconds of the blow. - Patient is pulseless, apneic, and unresponsive.

Prognosis

- Very high mortality (80–85%). - Best outcomes occur with immediate CPR and early defibrillation (AED).

Prevention

- Use of protective chest gear in high-risk sports. - Availability of AEDs at all athletic events.
